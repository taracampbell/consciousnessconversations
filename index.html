Conversations on Consciousness

October 20th, 2014

About

Conversations on Consciousness, is a student-organized interdisciplinary conference that aims to encourage a dialogue between experts in diverse fields with an interest in conscious experience. This October 20th, 2014 will be our inaugural year and we will address two themes: 1) How should our knowledge about consciousness guide our treatment of animals? and 2) What can the investigation of subjective experience, i.e., meditation, teach us about consciousness? Our speakers will include cognitive scientist and vegan activist Stevan Harnad, religious studies scholar and vegan activist Marion Achoulias, Philip Buckley, a phenomenologist and Ajahn Brahm, an ex-theoretical physicist and Buddhist monk.

In the spirit of conversation, each theme will first be addressed by two speakers, drawing from the perspectives of their discipline, before a conversation involving all speakers and members of the audience will be encouraged. Between sessions, Ajahn Brahm will provide meditation instruction and lead a sitting. All conference attendees are invited to experience, first-hand, the practice of meditation before enjoying a vegan lunch. Registration is $5.00 in person and $6.29 online.

Speakers

Listen to and participate in the conversations between a philosopher, cognitive scientist, buddhist monk and one more.

Marion Achoulias

Ajahn Brahm

Ajahn Brahm renounced the hallowed halls of Cambridge University where he studied theoretical physics to travel to Thailand and ordain as a Buddhist monk. His book “Mindfulness, Bliss and Beyond”, serves as a guidebook to those interested in rigorously pursuing the practice of meditation in order to glean insights about the nature of the mind. His years of meditation practice make him a unique authority on the possibilities of principled introspection as a tool for understanding the mind.

Philip Buckley

Prof. Buckley is former Chair of the Department of Philosophy. From 1986 to 1992 he was a scientific assistant of the Husserl- Archives at Louvain, which engages in editing and translating the unpublished works of Edmund Husserl. His research is focused upon the phenomenology of Husserl and Heidegger, with an interest also in its historical antecedents and subsequent developments in 20th century French and German philosophy. He is the coordinator of a FQRSC (Fonds quebecois de recherche sur la societé et la culture funded) research team for 2002-2005 with colleagues from the Université de Montréal on "Phenomenology and Ethics."

Stevan Harnad

Stevan Harnad is the Canada Research Chair in Cognitive Science. His passion for arguing that investigating the mind by building machines indistinguishable in function from human minds (“Turing Testing”) is the only feasible method available for understanding the mind is surpassed only by his indefatigable defense of animal rights. Stevan was a student of the late D. O. Hebb, whose conceptual postulate linking experience and neuronal properties provides the foundation by which we link mind and brain. Our interest in starting a conversation between experts in various disciplines was inspired by the journal founded by Steven Harnad, Brain and Behavioural Sciences, which provides a forum for dialogue between eminent scientists on controversial and novel ideas in the sciences of mind.
